Lin Hu Advanced Orthodontics!

Welcome to Dr. Lin Hu's practice. Dr. Hu is an ABO board certified orthodontist in greater New York who was recertified in 2008. Long Island office is located at Great Neck Plaza, a seven-minute walk from the LIRR Great Neck train station. It is convenient for the local residents and people coming from the other part of Long Island, as well as those who come from Little Neck and Bayside while driving and parking is much easier for them to this location. Our top priority is to provide you the best orthodontic care in a friendly, comfortable environment. We utilize the latest technological advances in the industry, such as invisible ceramic braces and Invisalign, along with the latest in computer technology (digital imaging and advanced computer graphics) to ensure that you receive the most effective care possible. We recognize that every patient has different needs, and we pride ourselves in the courteous service we deliver to each person who walks through our doors. Whether you are an adult, adolescent or child, our knowledgeable doctor and staff are committed to helping you achieve the smile you deserve - a healthy, beautiful one!

Your First Visit

We are always excited for your first visit to our office. This gives us a chance to get to know you. You will meet our Treatment Coordinator who will answer any questions you might have and find out your specific concerns. She will also take some photographs of your teeth that will be used to diagnose and discuss your orthodontic problems. Next, Dr. Hu will do an extensive examination and determine what type of treatment would benefit you most. By the end of your first visit, we want you to feel at home in our office and have confidence in our ability to provide excellent care and the best service. It is of utmost importance to our team that your experience on the road to a beautiful, healthy smile is as enjoyable for you as it is for us!

When a person's teeth or jaw structure do not fit together properly, orthodontic treatment may be necessary to straight the teeth and promote the ideal function.
